<TD WIDTH="9%" VALIGN="top" ALIGN="left"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2"><B>Item&nbsp;5.02</B></FONT></TD>
<TD ALIGN="left" VALIGN="top"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2"><B>Departure of Directors or Certain Officers; Election of Directors; Appointment of Certain Officers; Compensatory Arrangements of Certain Officers.
</B></FONT></TD></TR></TABLE> <P STYLE="margin-top:6px;margin-bottom:0px; text-indent:9%"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">(e) Compensatory Arrangements of Certain Officers. </FONT></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6px;margin-bottom:0px; text-indent:9%"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">Effective June&nbsp;21, 2013, the Compensation Committee (the &#147;Committee&#148;) of the Board of Directors of Apple Inc.
(&#147;we,&#148; &#147;Apple,&#148; or the &#147;Company&#148;) approved the amendment of the restricted stock units awarded on August&nbsp;24, 2011 to Timothy D. Cook, the Company&#146;s Chief Executive Officer. The amendment does not change the
original grant date fair value of Mr.&nbsp;Cook&#146;s award as originally reported in the Company&#146;s Proxy Statement filed with the SEC on January&nbsp;9, 2012. It does, however, align Mr.&nbsp;Cook&#146;s potential realizable compensation from
the award with Company performance, and reflects the Committee&#146;s intent to have a portion of future equity awards be performance-based for the Company&#146;s executive officers, and for Mr.&nbsp;Cook to lead by example. </FONT></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:18px;margin-bottom:0px; text-indent:9%"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2"><B>Move to Performance-Based Equity </B></FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6px;margin-bottom:0px; text-indent:9%"><FONT
STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">Apple continues to enhance its corporate governance in a variety of ways. Since 2011, we have adopted majority voting for directors, advocated the elimination of blank check preferred stock, and
implemented robust stock ownership guidelines for our CEO and the other named executive officers and directors. In each of the past three years, our shareholders have decisively approved the say-on-pay proposals for our executive compensation
program. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px; text-indent:9%"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">In outreach discussions this year with many of our largest shareholders, we heard that they believe it is
appropriate to attach performance criteria to a portion of our future executive stock awards that have been entirely time-based (i.e., vesting for continued service) in the past. We agree and, beginning today, the Company will include a performance
element in new stock awards to our executive officers. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:18px;margin-bottom:0px; text-indent:9%"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2"><B>CEO Leadership by Example </B></FONT></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6px;margin-bottom:0px; text-indent:9%"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">Mr.&nbsp;Cook is leading this initiative by example and has the full support of the Board of Directors. He asked the Committee to apply a
performance metric to his outstanding 2011 CEO equity award as well as any potential future awards. After careful deliberation, the Committee has approved a modification to Mr.&nbsp;Cook&#146;s 2011 award. </FONT></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px; text-indent:9%"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">Under the adopted modification, Mr.&nbsp;Cook will forfeit a portion of the 2011 CEO equity award, which was previously entirely
time-based, if the Company does not achieve certain performance criteria. While the Committee generally believes that a performance-based award should have both a downside and an upside component, at Mr.&nbsp;Cook&#146;s request, the modification
does not contain an upside opportunity for overachievement of these criteria. As a result of implementing a modification with only downside risk, the Committee has determined that a portion of the original grant should vest earlier than originally
scheduled. This modification will not change the award&#146;s original value for accounting expense purposes. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:18px;margin-bottom:0px; text-indent:9%"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2"><B>Performance
Measurement and Shareholder Alignment </B></FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6px;margin-bottom:0px; text-indent:9%"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">After careful deliberation and consultation with shareholders and compensation
experts, the Committee has concluded that relative &#147;total shareholder return&#148; (&#147;TSR&#148;) should be most relevant to Apple shareholders in evaluating Apple&#146;s performance against that of other companies.* </FONT></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:18px;margin-bottom:0px; text-indent:9%"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2"><B>2011 CEO Equity Award Modification </B></FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6px;margin-bottom:0px; text-indent:9%"><FONT
STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">As a result of the adoption of a performance component and regular performance measurements, and the absence of a performance multiplier, the Committee has modified Mr.&nbsp;Cook&#146;s 2011 award of one
million RSUs to vest as follows: 100,000 RSUs remain scheduled to vest on August&nbsp;24, 2016; 100,000 RSUs remain scheduled to vest on August&nbsp;24, 2021; the balance of 800,000 RSUs is separated into ten equal tranches of 80,000 RSUs each that
vest over the ten-year life of the award. Details are explained below and illustrated in Table 1. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px; text-indent:9%"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">The Committee considered
what percentage of Mr.&nbsp;Cook&#146;s unvested one-year RSU tranches to place at risk under the new performance criteria. Because Mr.&nbsp;Cook faces only downside risk from the modification, the Committee believed that less than 50% should be
placed at risk. Mr.&nbsp;Cook, however, expressed a strong desire to set a leadership example in&nbsp;the area of CEO compensation and governance&nbsp;and requested a larger at-risk percentage.&nbsp;Accordingly,&nbsp;the Committee is&nbsp;placing
50% of the RSUs at risk in each future annual performance-based tranche. </FONT></P>

<p Style='page-break-before:always'>
<HR  SIZE="3" style="COLOR:#999999" WIDTH="100%" ALIGN="CENTER">
 <P STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px; text-indent:9%"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">The relative TSR criteria will be applied to each 80,000 RSU tranche scheduled to vest on
each anniversary of the original August&nbsp;24, 2011 grant date, and will compare Apple&#146;s TSR to the TSR of the companies in the S&amp;P 500 using public data derived from Standard and Poor&#146;s. If Apple&#146;s performance is within the top
third of that group, the RSUs in the tranche for that year will vest in full. If its performance is in the middle third, the RSUs in the tranche for that year will be reduced by 25%, and if its performance is in the bottom third, the RSUs in that
tranche will be reduced by 50%. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px; text-indent:9%"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">In considering the time frame to measure relative TSR, the Committee determined, after
consultation with compensation experts and shareholders, to use a three-year period and to ramp up to this three-year period starting with the tranche vesting in 2014. Therefore, the tranche vesting in August 2014 will be measured against a one-year
TSR period. The tranche vesting in August 2015 will use a two-year period. The tranches vesting in 2016 and beyond will use a full three-year period. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px; text-indent:9%"><FONT
STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">Because this modification takes place in 2013, partway through the vesting period of the original 2011 grant, the Committee separately considered the treatment of the first two tranches that, if modified
earlier, would have vested in August 2012 and August 2013. For the tranche that would have vested in August 2012, the RSUs will vest in full on the modification date. This would have been the result whether the Committee had applied a one-, two- or
three-year relative TSR measurement in August 2012. For the tranche vesting in August 2013, the Committee will vest the portion from August&nbsp;25, 2012 until the modification date on a time basis and will measure the remaining portion of the
tranche between the modification date and the August 2013 anniversary against the full year&#146;s relative TSR criteria. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:18px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2"><B>Table 1
