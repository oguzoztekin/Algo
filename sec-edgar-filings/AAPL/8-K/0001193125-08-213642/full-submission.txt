 <BODY BGCOLOR="WHITE">

 <P STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px" ALIGN="right"><FONT FACE="Times New Roman" SIZE="2"><B>Exhibit 99.1 </B></FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT FACE="Times New Roman"
SIZE="3"><B>Apple Reports Fourth Quarter Results </B></FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT FACE="Times New Roman" SIZE="2"><B>6.9 Million iPhones Sold </B></FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT
FACE="Times New Roman" SIZE="2"><B>Mac Sales Reach All-Time High </B></FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6px;margin-bottom:0px;padding-bottom:3px;line-height:95%; vertical-align:top"><FONT FACE="Times New Roman" SIZE="2">CUPERTINO,
California&#151;October 21, 2008&#151;Apple<FONT FACE="Times New Roman" SIZE="1"><SUP>&reg;</SUP></FONT> today announced financial results for its fiscal 2008 fourth quarter ended September&nbsp;27, 2008. The Company posted revenue of $7.9 billion
and net quarterly profit of $1.14 billion, or $1.26 per diluted share. These results compare to revenue of $6.22 billion and net quarterly profit of $904 million, or $1.01 per diluted share, in the year-ago quarter. Gross margin was 34.7 percent, up
from 33.6 percent in the year-ago quarter. International sales accounted for 41 percent of the quarter&#146;s revenue. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px;padding-bottom:3px;line-height:95%; vertical-align:top"><FONT
FACE="Times New Roman" SIZE="2">In accordance with the subscription accounting treatment required by GAAP, the Company recognizes revenue and cost of goods sold for iPhone&#153; and Apple TV<FONT FACE="Times New Roman"
SIZE="1"><SUP>&reg;</SUP></FONT> over their economic lives. Adjusting GAAP sales and product costs to eliminate the impact of subscription accounting, the corresponding non-GAAP measures* for the quarter are $11.68 billion of &#147;Adjusted
Sales&#148; and $2.44 billion of &#147;Adjusted Net Income.&#148; </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px;padding-bottom:3px;line-height:95%; vertical-align:top"><FONT FACE="Times New Roman" SIZE="2">Apple shipped 2,611,000
Macintosh<FONT FACE="Times New Roman" SIZE="1"><SUP>&reg;</SUP></FONT> computers during the quarter, representing 21 percent unit growth and 17 percent revenue growth over the year-ago quarter. The Company sold 11,052,000 iPods during the quarter,
representing eight percent unit growth and three percent revenue growth over the year-ago quarter. Quarterly iPhone units sold were 6,892,000 compared to 1,119,000 in the year-ago-quarter. </FONT></P> <P
STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT FACE="Times New Roman" SIZE="2">&#147;Apple just reported one of the best quarters in its history, with a spectacular performance by the iPhone&#151;we sold more phones than RIM,&#148; said Steve Jobs,
Apple&#146;s CEO. &#147;We don&#146;t yet know how this economic downturn will affect Apple. But we&#146;re armed with the strongest product line in our history, the most talented employees and the best customers in our industry. And $25 billion of
cash safely in the bank with zero debt.&#148; </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT FACE="Times New Roman" SIZE="2">&#147;We&#146;re very pleased to have grown revenue 35 percent and to have generated $9.1 billion in cash in
fiscal 2008,&#148; said Peter Oppenheimer, Apple&#146;s CFO. &#147;Looking ahead, visibility is low and forecasting is challenging, and as a result we are going to be prudent in predicting the December quarter. We are providing a wide range for our
guidance, targeting revenue of $9.0 to $10.0 billion and earnings per diluted share between $1.06 and $1.35.&#148; </FONT></P>

 <P STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px;padding-bottom:3px;line-height:95%; vertical-align:top"><FONT FACE="Times New Roman" SIZE="2">Apple will provide live streaming of its Q4 2008 financial results
conference call utilizing QuickTime<FONT FACE="Times New Roman" SIZE="1"><SUP>&reg;</SUP></FONT>, Apple&#146;s standards-based technology for live and on-demand audio and video streaming. The live webcast will begin at 2:00 p.m. PDT on Tuesday,
October&nbsp;21, 2008 at <U>www.apple.com/quicktime/qtv/earningsq408/</U> and will also be available for replay for approximately two weeks thereafter. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:18px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT FACE="Times New Roman"
SIZE="2"><B>*Non-GAAP Financial Measures </B></FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT FACE="Times New Roman" SIZE="2">During fiscal 2007, the Company began selling the iPhone and Apple TV. Because the Company may provide
unspecified features and additional software products to iPhone and Apple TV customers in the future free of charge, in accordance with GAAP the Company recognizes revenue and cost of goods sold for these products on a straight-line basis over their
economic lives, with any loss recognized at the time of sale. Currently, the economic lives of these products are estimated to be 24 months. This accounting treatment, referred to as subscription accounting, results in the deferral of almost all of
the revenue and cost of goods during the quarter in which the products are sold to the customer. Other costs related to these products, including costs for engineering, sales, marketing and warranty, are expensed as incurred. Further, the costs to
develop any future unspecified features and additional software products that may eventually be provided to customers also are expensed as incurred. In contrast, the Company generally recognizes revenue and cost of goods sold for its other products,
such as Macs and iPods, at the time of sale, as the Company does not provide future unspecified features or additional software products to those customers free of charge. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT
FACE="Times New Roman" SIZE="2">In July 2008, the Company began selling iPhone 3G, the second-generation iPhone, and significantly expanded distribution by establishing carrier relationships in over 70 countries. Unit sales of iPhone 3G have been
significantly greater than sales of the first-generation iPhone. During the first quarter of iPhone 3G availability ended September&nbsp;27, 2008, 6.9&nbsp;million units were sold, exceeding the 6.1&nbsp;million first-generation iPhone units sold in
the prior five quarters combined. As a result of this growth in unit sales, the amount of iPhone revenue and product cost that the Company deferred for recognition in future periods under subscription accounting increased materially in the quarter
ended September&nbsp;27, 2008. </FONT></P>

<p Style='page-break-before:always'>
<HR  SIZE="3" COLOR="#999999" WIDTH="100%" ALIGN="CENTER">

 <P STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT FACE="Times New Roman" SIZE="2">While the GAAP results provide significant insight into the Company&#146;s operations and financial position, management
supplements its analysis of the business using financial measures that look at the total sales, related product costs and resulting income for iPhones and Apple TVs sold to customers during the period. The presentation at the end of this press
release includes the following non-GAAP measures: &#147;Adjusted Sales,&#148; &#147;Adjusted Cost of Sales,&#148; &#147;Adjusted Gross Margin,&#148; &#147;Adjusted Operating Margin,&#148; &#147;Adjusted Income before Provision for Income
Taxes,&#148; &#147;Adjusted Provision for Income Taxes,&#148; &#147;Adjusted Net Income&#148; and &#147;Adjusted Diluted Earnings per Share.&#148; These financial measures are not consistent with GAAP because they do not reflect the deferral of
revenue and product costs for recognition in later periods. The Company uses these financial measures, along with other measures discussed below, to provide additional insight into current operating and business trends not readily apparent from the
GAAP results. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT FACE="Times New Roman" SIZE="2">Management uses Adjusted Sales to evaluate the Company&#146;s growth rate, revenue mix and performance relative to competitors. Given the
significant increase in iPhone unit sales during the quarter ended September&nbsp;27, 2008, Adjusted Sales provides a meaningful measurement of the Company&#146;s growth by reflecting amounts generally due to Apple at the time of sale related to
products sold within the period. Further, eliminating the effects of deferred revenue (current sales deferred to future periods and prior sales being recognized currently) provides more transparency into the Company&#146;s underlying sales trends.
Management uses the non-GAAP measures of &#147;Adjusted Cost of Sales,&#148; &#147;Adjusted Gross Margin&#148; and &#147;Adjusted Operating Margin&#148; to measure the Company&#146;s operating performance based on current period iPhone and Apple TV
sales and to facilitate on-going operating decisions. Additionally, because the Company recognizes engineering, sales, and marketing expenses as incurred, including expenses related to iPhone and Apple TV, management uses Adjusted Sales to evaluate
returns on those costs, to manage year-over-year operating expense growth, and to budget future expenses. </FONT>
</P>

&#147;Adjusted Operating Margin&#148; are metrics that will factor into the determination of management compensation beginning in fiscal year 2009. Finally, management uses the non-GAAP measures of &#147;Adjusted Income before Provision for Income
Taxes,&#148; &#147;Adjusted Provision for Income Taxes,&#148; &#147;Adjusted Net Income&#148; and &#147;Adjusted Diluted Earnings per Share&#148; to measure the Company&#146;s operating performance based on current period iPhone and Apple TV sales,
to facilitate on-going operating decisions, and compare performance relative to competitors. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT FACE="Times New Roman" SIZE="2">Management believes that these non-GAAP financial measures,
when taken together with the corresponding consolidated GAAP measures and related segment information, provide incremental insight into the underlying factors and trends affecting both the Company&#146;s performance and its cash generating
potential. Management believes these non-GAAP measures increase the transparency of the Company&#146;s current results and enable investors to more fully understand trends in its current and future performance. Beginning with this earnings release,
the Company plans to include these non-GAAP measures of financial performance as part of its earnings releases. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:18px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT FACE="Times New Roman" SIZE="2"><B>Cautions on Use of Non-GAAP Measures
</B></FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT FACE="Times New Roman" SIZE="2">As noted previously, these non-GAAP financial measures are not consistent with GAAP because they do not reflect the deferral of revenue and product
costs for recognition in later periods. These non-GAAP financial measures do not adjust for the costs associated with the Company&#146;s intention to provide unspecified new features and software to purchasers of iPhone and Apple TV products. These
costs are expensed as incurred under GAAP&#146;s subscription accounting model, and are not adjusted in these non-GAAP financial measures. As such, these non-GAAP financial measures are not intended to reflect in a given period all of the costs of
sales made in that period. Rather, the non-GAAP financial measures presented below are intended for the limited purpose of presenting performance measures that include the total sales, related product costs, and resulting income for iPhones and
AppleTVs in the period those products are sold to customers. </FONT></P>
