Mac&nbsp;&amp; App Store Drive Revenue Growth of 33% </B></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:9pt; font-family:ARIAL" ALIGN="justify">CUPERTINO, California&#151;July&nbsp;21, 2015&#151;Apple<SUP
STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">&reg;</SUP> today announced financial results for its fiscal 2015 third quarter ended June&nbsp;27, 2015. The Company posted quarterly revenue of $49.6&nbsp;billion and quarterly net profit of
$10.7&nbsp;billion, or $1.85 per diluted share. These results compare to revenue of $37.4&nbsp;billion and net profit of $7.7&nbsp;billion, or $1.28 per diluted share, in the year-ago quarter. Gross margin was 39.7 percent compared to 39.4 percent
in the year-ago quarter. International sales accounted for 64 percent of the quarter&#146;s revenue. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:9pt; font-family:ARIAL" ALIGN="justify">The growth was fueled by record third quarter

<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:9pt; font-family:ARIAL" ALIGN="justify">&#147;We had an amazing quarter, with iPhone revenue up 59 percent over last year, strong sales of Mac, all-time record revenue from services, driven by
the App Store, and a great start for Apple Watch,&#148; said Tim Cook, Apple&#146;s CEO. &#147;The excitement for Apple Music has been incredible, and we&#146;re looking forward to releasing iOS 9, OS X El Capitan and watchOS 2 to customers in the
fall.&#148; </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:9pt; font-family:ARIAL" ALIGN="justify">&#147;In the third quarter our year-over-year growth rate accelerated from the first half of fiscal 2015, with revenue up 33 percent and
earnings per share up 45 percent,&#148; said Luca Maestri, Apple&#146;s CFO. &#147;We generated very strong operating cash flow of $15&nbsp;billion, and we returned over $13&nbsp;billion to shareholders through our capital return program.&#148; </P>
