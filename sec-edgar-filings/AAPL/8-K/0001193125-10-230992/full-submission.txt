 <P STYLE="font-size:1px;margin-top:18px;margin-bottom:0px">&nbsp;</P> <P STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px" ALIGN="right"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2"><B>Exhibit 99.1
</B></FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="3"><B>Apple Reports Fourth Quarter Results </B></FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT
STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2"><B>Record Mac, iPhone and iPad Sales </B></FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2"><B>Highest Revenue and Earnings Ever </B></FONT></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6px;margin-bottom:0px;padding-bottom:0px;"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">CUPERTINO, California&#151;October 18, 2010&#151;Apple<FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="1"><SUP
STYLE="vertical-align:baseline; position:relative; bottom:.8ex">&reg;</SUP></FONT> today announced financial results for its fiscal 2010 fourth quarter ended September&nbsp;25, 2010. The Company posted record revenue of $20.34 billion and net
quarterly profit of $4.31 billion, or $4.64 per diluted share. These results compare to revenue of $12.21 billion and net quarterly profit of $2.53 billion, or $2.77 per diluted share, in the year-ago quarter. Gross margin was 36.9 percent compared
to 41.8 percent in the year-ago quarter. International sales accounted for 57 percent of the quarter&#146;s revenue. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">Apple sold
3.89&nbsp;million Macs during the quarter, a 27 percent unit increase over the year-ago quarter. The Company sold 14.1&nbsp;million iPhones in the quarter, representing 91 percent unit growth over the year-ago quarter. Apple sold 9.05&nbsp;million
iPods during the quarter, representing an 11 percent unit decline from the year-ago quarter. The Company also sold 4.19&nbsp;million iPads during the quarter. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT
STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">&#147;We are blown away to report over $20 billion in revenue and over $4 billion in after-tax earnings&#151;both all-time records for Apple,&#148; said Steve Jobs, Apple&#146;s CEO. &#147;iPhone sales of
14.1&nbsp;million were up 91 percent year-over-year, handily beating the 12.1&nbsp;million phones RIM sold in their most recent quarter. We still have a few surprises left for the remainder of this calendar year.&#148; </FONT></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">&#147;We&#146;re thrilled with the performance and strength of our business, generating almost $5.7 billion in cash flow from operations during the
quarter,&#148; said Peter Oppenheimer, Apple&#146;s CFO. &#147;Looking ahead to the first fiscal quarter of 2011, we expect revenue of about $23 billion and we expect diluted earnings per share of about $4.80.&#148; </FONT></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">Apple will provide live streaming of its Q4 2010 financial results conference call beginning at 2:00 p.m. PDT on October&nbsp;18, 2010 at
<U>www.apple.com/quicktime/qtv/earningsq410/</U>. This webcast will also be available for replay for approximately two weeks thereafter. </FONT></P>
