<p style="margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">On August 29, 2006, Apple
Computer, Inc. (the &#147;Company&#148;) appointed Dr. Eric Schmidt to its Board of
Directors (the &#147;Board&#148;).&#160; A copy of the
press release issued by the Company on August 29, 2006, announcing Dr. Schmidt&#146;s
appointment to the Board, is attached hereto as Exhibit 99.1.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-justify:inter-ideograph;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">There are no relationships or related transactions between Dr. Schmidt
and the Company that would be required to be reported under Section 404(a) of
Regulation S-K. Dr. Schmidt has been appointed to the Special Committee of the
Board. In connection with his appointment, Dr. Schmidt has declined the
automatic stock option grant to purchase 30,000 shares to which new directors
are entitled under the Company&#146;s 1997 Director Stock Option Plan, and instead
intends to purchase 10,000 shares of the Company&#146;s common stock on the open
market.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-justify:inter-ideograph;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">In connection with the appointment, the Company entered into an
indemnification agreement with Dr. Schmidt, the terms of which are identical in
all material respects to the form of indemnification agreement that the Company
has previously entered into with each of its directors, which was filed with
the SEC as Exhibit 10.A.8 to the Company&#146;s Annual Report on Form 10-K for the
fiscal year ended September 26, 1997.</font></p>

<p align="center" style="margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-align:center;"><b><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">Google

<p style="margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">CUPERTINO,
California&#151;August 29, 2006 &#151;Apple&#174; today announced that Dr. Eric Schmidt, chief
executive officer of Google, was elected to Apple&#146;s board of directors at their
meeting today. Eric also sits on Google&#146;s board of directors and Princeton
University&#146;s board of trustees.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&#147;Eric is obviously doing

experience will be very valuable in helping to guide Apple in the years ahead.&#148;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&#147;Apple is one of the
companies in the world that I most admire,&#148; said Eric Schmidt. &#147;I&#146;m really
looking forward to working with Steve and Apple&#146;s board to help with all of the
amazing things Apple is doing.&#148;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Eric Schmidt joined
Google from Novell, where he was chairman and CEO. At Google, Eric shares
responsibility for Google&#146;s day-to-day operations, and is focused on building
the corporate infrastructure needed to maintain Google&#146;s rapid growth while
ensuring that quality remains high and product development times are kept to a
minimum. Eric&#146;s 20 years of experience as an Internet strategist, entrepreneur
and technologist give him a well-seasoned perspective which perfectly
complements Google&#146;s needs as a young and rapidly growing company with a unique
corporate culture.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Prior to joining Novell,
Eric was chief technology officer at Sun Microsystems, Inc., where he led the
development of Java, Sun&#146;s platform-independent programming technology, and
defined Sun&#146;s Internet software strategy. Before joining Sun in 1983, he was a
member of the research staff at the Computer Science Lab at Xerox Palo Alto
Research Center (PARC), and held positions at Bell Laboratories and Zilog. Eric
has a bachelor of science degree in electrical engineering from Princeton
University, and a master&#146;s and Ph.D. in computer science from the University of
California-Berkeley. In 2006, Eric was elected to the National Academy of
Engineering, which recognized his work on &#147;the development of strategies for
the world&#146;s most successful Internet search engine company.&#148;</font></p>
